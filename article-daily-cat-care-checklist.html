Everyday Cat Care

Daily Cat Care Checklist

Cats thrive on routine. This simple daily, weekly, monthly and yearly checklist keeps feeding, litter, play, grooming and health checks on track — so your cat stays happy and problems get caught early.

⏱️ 8 min read
Updated 8 July 2026
First published 8 July 2026
Reviewed for Cat&Me Care

Good cat care isn't about doing more — it's about doing a few key things consistently. Cats are creatures of habit who feel safest when meals, play and cleaning happen on a predictable rhythm. The bonus for you: a routine turns care into a handful of quick, easy tasks, and makes it obvious the moment something is off. Here's a checklist broken down by how often each job needs doing.

💡 Why routine matters

Cats can be genuinely stressed by change. A steady daily rhythm reduces anxiety, supports good litter habits, and gives you a baseline — so a skipped meal or a change in toileting stands out as an early warning sign.

Every day

These are the non-negotiables — the small tasks that keep your cat fed, clean, active and content.

  • Feed measured meals at regular times for their age and weight
  • Refresh water — wash the bowl and refill with clean water daily
  • Scoop the litter box (once or twice), and top up litter
  • Play and interact for 10–15 minutes, ideally in a couple of sessions
  • Give attention and affection — talk to, stroke or sit with your cat
  • Do a quick “eyeball” health check: appetite, energy, toileting and mood

✓ Play with a purpose

Use wand toys or a ball to let your cat “hunt”. A short burst of chasing and pouncing before a meal satisfies their predator instincts, burns energy, and helps prevent boredom behaviours and night-time zoomies.

Every week

  • Brush your cat (daily for long coats; weekly is fine for short coats)
  • Wash food and water bowls thoroughly
  • Check nails and trim the tips if needed
  • Wipe down the feeding area and around the litter box
  • Rotate or tidy toys to keep things interesting
  • Vacuum fur and check scratching posts

Every month

  • Deep-clean litter boxes (full empty, wash and refill)
  • Weigh your cat and note any gain or loss
  • Check ears, eyes, teeth and gums; look and feel for lumps
  • Give flea (and, if advised, worm) treatment on schedule
  • Wash cat beds, blankets and soft bedding
  • Restock food, litter and any medicines

⚠️ Watch for changes

Contact your vet if you notice ongoing changes in appetite, drinking, weight, litter habits, energy or grooming — or any vomiting, diarrhoea or lethargy lasting more than a day or two. Cats hide illness well, so trust your monthly checks.

Every year

  • Book an annual vet check-up (twice a year for senior cats)
  • Keep vaccinations and parasite prevention up to date
  • Confirm microchip details are correct and current
  • Review diet and portions as your cat ages or its weight changes
  • Reassess enrichment — new toys, posts or climbing spaces

Making the routine stick

  • Tie tasks to your own habits — scoop litter when you make coffee, play before dinner.
  • Share the load — a family chart means everyone helps and everyone notices changes.
  • Keep supplies handy — scoop, brush and treats within reach make the jobs effortless.
  • Adapt to your cat — kittens, seniors and cats with health needs may need more frequent care.

✓ The takeaway

Feed, water, scoop, play and check in every day; groom and tidy weekly; do a health-and-clean sweep monthly; and see the vet yearly. That simple rhythm keeps your cat healthy and content — and turns you into the person who spots trouble before it grows.

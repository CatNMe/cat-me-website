Best Cat Breeds for Families

Some cat breeds are famous for being patient, sociable and gentle enough to thrive in a busy home with children and other pets. Here are the family favourites — and why an individual cat's personality still matters more than any breed label.

⏱️ 8 min read
Updated 8 July 2026
First published 8 July 2026
Reviewed for Cat&Me

There are more than 70 recognised cat breeds, and many are known for warm, people-loving personalities. If you have children or other pets, some breeds tend to cope better with the noise, handling and unpredictability of family life. Below are breeds that regularly top the “best with kids” lists — but remember the golden rule: every cat is an individual, and how a cat is raised and socialised often matters more than its breed.

💡 Breed is a guide, not a guarantee

Breed traits describe tendencies, not certainties. A well-socialised moggy (mixed-breed cat) from a shelter can make just as wonderful a family pet as any pedigree — and adoption gives a cat in need a home.

What makes a good family cat?

Before looking at breeds, it helps to know the traits that suit family life:

  • Tolerance and patience — comfortable being handled, and forgiving of noise and sudden movements.
  • Sociability — enjoys company and doesn't mind a busy, people-filled home.
  • Playfulness — happy to join in games, which children love.
  • Adaptability — settles into new routines, visitors and other pets at their own pace.
  • Steady temperament — not easily startled into scratching or hiding.

Family-friendly breeds to consider

These breeds are repeatedly recommended by vets, breeders and pet organisations as good matches for families.

Gentle and laid-back: Ragdoll & British Shorthair

If you'd like a calm, cuddly companion, the Ragdoll and British Shorthair are hard to beat. Ragdolls are so relaxed they often flop in your arms, and British Shorthairs are unflappable and independent — happy to be near the family without demanding constant attention.

Sociable and playful: Maine Coon, Burmese & Abyssinian

For active households that want an interactive cat, these breeds bring energy and fun. Maine Coons are dog-like and playful, Burmese love a busy home, and Abyssinians are curious explorers who want to be part of everything.

Don't overlook adoption

✓ Meet the cat, not just the breed

Shelters are full of friendly, family-ready cats of every age. Ask staff which cats are known to be relaxed with children and other pets — they see each cat's real personality every day. An adult cat's temperament is also already visible, which takes the guesswork out.

Setting kids and cats up for success

  • Teach children to be gentle and to let the cat approach them
  • Show kids the signs a cat wants space (flattened ears, twitching tail, hiding)
  • Never disturb a cat while it's eating, sleeping or using the litter tray
  • Give the cat a quiet, child-free retreat it can always escape to
  • Supervise young children and cats during early interactions
  • Involve kids in gentle care like feeding and play to build a bond

✓ The takeaway

Ragdolls, Maine Coons, British Shorthairs, Birmans and Burmese all have reputations as brilliant family cats — but temperament, socialisation and gentle handling matter more than any breed name. Choose the individual cat that suits your home, and teach children to respect it.

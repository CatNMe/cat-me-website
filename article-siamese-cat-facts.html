Siamese Cat Facts

With their piercing blue eyes, elegant colour-point coats and famously talkative nature, Siamese cats are one of the world's oldest and most instantly recognisable breeds. Here's what makes a “meezer” so unique.

⏱️ 8 min read
Updated 8 July 2026
First published 8 July 2026
Reviewed for Cat&Me

Siamese cats are cats of extremes — long, slender lines, a striking triangular face, and a personality every bit as bold as their looks. Affectionately nicknamed “meezers,” they're loving, clever and endlessly chatty. Here are the facts behind this legendary breed.

💡 Quick summary

Origin: Thailand (formerly Siam). Size: medium, slender, muscular. Coat: short, fine, colour-pointed. Eyes: always blue. Temperament: highly social, vocal, intelligent. Lifespan: often into the mid-teens or beyond.

1. One of the oldest known cat breeds

The Siamese comes from Thailand, formerly known as Siam. It's one of the oldest and first distinctly recognised breeds of domestic cat, appearing in an ancient Thai manuscript called the Tamra Maew (“The Cat-Book Poems”), thought to date back several centuries. Siamese cats were prized by royalty and temples, which may explain their air of self-importance! They were officially recognised in the West in the early 1900s and quickly became one of the most popular pedigree cats.

2. Blue eyes and colour points

The Siamese has a short, glossy coat that lies close to its slender body, with a lighter body colour and darker “points” on the ears, face (mask), legs, paws and tail. Their almond-shaped eyes are always a vivid blue.

Classic point coloursAppearance
Seal pointDeep brown, almost black points on a cream body
Chocolate pointWarm milk-chocolate points on an ivory body
Blue pointCool slate-grey points on a bluish-white body
Lilac pointPinkish-grey points on a pale, frosty body

✓ Why the points? A temperature trick

Siamese kittens are born almost pure white. Their colour is temperature-sensitive — the darker points develop on the cooler parts of the body (ears, face, paws, tail), while the warmer body stays pale. Cats in cooler climates often develop darker coats.

3. Vocal, social and highly intelligent

If you want a quiet, aloof cat, the Siamese is not for you. These are among the most people-oriented of all breeds — they crave company, follow their owners everywhere, and “supervise” everything you do. They're also famously vocal, holding loud, insistent “conversations” with a distinctive raspy meow.

Siamese cats are often considered one of the most intelligent breeds. They can learn tricks, walk on a harness, and solve puzzle feeders — but that cleverness means they need plenty of stimulation, and they don't like being ignored or left alone for long.

⚠️ Not a “leave them alone” cat

Siamese thrive on interaction and can become stressed, bored or destructive if left alone too much. Many owners keep them in pairs or ensure plenty of daily play and company.

4. Care & grooming

Their short, fine coat is wonderfully low-maintenance — a weekly brush or even a quick stroke with a damp hand is usually enough to keep it glossy. Beyond that, the Siamese needs what all cats need: good food, fresh water, clean litter, and — crucially for this breed — lots of mental and physical enrichment.

5. Health & lifespan

Siamese are often long-lived, frequently reaching their mid-teens and beyond with good care. Like all pedigree breeds, they can be predisposed to certain conditions, including respiratory issues, dental disease, some heart defects, and occasionally eye or neurological conditions. Choosing a responsible breeder and keeping up with regular vet check-ups helps keep them healthy.

Is a Siamese right for you?

  • You want an affectionate, interactive, “velcro” companion
  • You don't mind — or actively enjoy — a talkative cat
  • Someone is home often, or the cat has feline company
  • You can provide daily play, puzzles and enrichment
  • You'd like a low-grooming, elegant short-haired cat

✓ The takeaway

The Siamese is a striking, ancient breed with blue eyes, colour points and a huge personality. Intelligent, loyal and extremely social, they make devoted companions — as long as you can give them the attention, interaction and mental stimulation they crave.

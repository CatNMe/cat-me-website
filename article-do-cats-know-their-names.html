Do Cats Know Their Names?

You call your cat and it stares at the wall. So does it actually recognise its name — or is it just ignoring you? Science has an answer, and it's more flattering to cats than you might think.

⏱️ 6 min read
Updated 8 July 2026
First published 8 July 2026
Reviewed for Cat&Me

The short answer is yes — studies show that many pet cats can distinguish their own name from other, similar-sounding words. They may not always show it, but the recognition is real.

💡 Quick summary

A 2019 study published in Scientific Reports found that domestic cats can tell their own name apart from similar words and other cats' names. Cats often respond subtly — an ear twitch or tail flick — rather than coming when called. Regular, positive use of a name strengthens recognition.

1. The science says yes

In 2019, a team led by researcher Atsuko Saito (then working with the University of Tokyo) published a study in the journal Scientific Reports titled “Domestic cats discriminate their names from other words.” It was the first experimental evidence that cats can understand something about human speech directed at them.

The conclusion: pet cats can recognise their own name, especially when it's used regularly by the people they live with. Previous work by the same lab had already shown cats can recognise their owner's voice and follow human pointing gestures.

2. How the researchers tested it

Cats don't sit exams, so the team used a clever method based on habituation. A person read out a series of four ordinary words that sounded similar to the cat's name. With each word, the cat's reaction faded — it grew bored and stopped reacting.

Then, as the fifth word, they said the cat's actual name. Many cats perked up again — moving their ears, turning their head, or shifting — showing they noticed a meaningful difference between the filler words and their own name.

✓ Clever design

The names were even tested by unfamiliar people, not just owners — and cats still reacted to their own name. That suggests they were responding to the sound pattern itself, not just a familiar voice.

3. So why do they ignore me?

Recognising a name and choosing to respond are two very different things. Unlike dogs, cats weren't bred over thousands of years to obey human commands, so they simply don't feel obliged to come running.

When a cat hears its name, it decides whether responding is worth it. Often the answer is a subtle acknowledgement — a flick of the ear or a lazy tail twitch — rather than getting up. That's not confusion; it's a cat weighing its options.

Subtle responseWhat it may mean
Ear twitch or rotation“I heard you” — acknowledgement without moving
Head turn toward youMild interest or curiosity
Tail flickRegistering the sound, deciding whether to act
Coming overStrong positive association — often name + reward

4. Names of other cats

The study also found that cats in ordinary homes could distinguish their own name from the names of other cats they lived with. Interestingly, cats from a cat café — surrounded by many cats and names — were less able to tell their own name from other resident cats' names, probably because names there weren't tied to clear individual outcomes.

5. How to help your cat learn its name

You can strengthen your cat's name recognition with simple, positive habits:

  • Say the name clearly, then immediately reward with a treat, meal or play
  • Keep the name consistent — avoid a dozen different nicknames early on
  • Use it in calm, positive moments, not just when telling them off
  • Never pair the name with punishment — it teaches them to tune it out
  • Be patient and keep sessions short and upbeat

💡 Tip

If you use the name only when it's time for something unpleasant — like nail trims or medicine — your cat may learn to associate it with things to avoid. Balance it with plenty of good experiences.

The takeaway

✓ In short

Your cat almost certainly knows its name — it just reserves the right not to react. Recognition is built through repetition and positive associations, so a name paired with treats, meals and affection is one your cat will happily notice.

Signs Your Cat Is Bored

Cats hide boredom well — but it leaks out in telltale ways, from over-sleeping to shredded furniture at 3am. Here's how to read the signs and turn things around.

⏱️ 8 min read
Updated 7 July 2026
First published 7 July 2026
Reviewed for Cat&Me Indoor Life

Cats sleep a lot — so it's easy to assume a quiet cat is a content one. But under-stimulated indoor cats genuinely get bored, and boredom is more than a mood: it's a welfare issue that can lead to stress, weight gain and problem behaviours. Because cats mask discomfort, boredom often shows up as something else — a shredded sofa, a 3am yowl, a suddenly food-obsessed cat. Here's how to tell the difference, and what to do about it.

💡 The short version

Common boredom signs include sleeping even more than usual, overeating, overgrooming, destructive scratching, attention-seeking, and night-time zoomies. First rule out illness with your vet, then fix boredom with daily play, vertical space, and food puzzles.

The main signs of boredom

💤 Sleeping even more than usual

Cats nap a lot normally, but a cat that's checked out — sleeping nearly all day with little alert, engaged time — may be under-stimulated rather than restful. Healthy cats have bursts of curiosity and activity.

🍽️ Overeating or weight gain

With nothing else to do, some cats eat for entertainment, leading to weight gain — while others lose interest in food. Any significant appetite change should also be checked by a vet to rule out a medical cause.

🧼 Overgrooming or repetitive behaviour

Bored or stressed cats may lick, chew or pull fur until there are bald patches or sore skin, creating a hard-to-break loop. Overgrooming can also signal pain, allergies or stress — so it always warrants a vet check.

🛋️ Destructive scratching & knocking things over

Shredding furniture, climbing curtains, or deliberately swatting objects off shelves — especially while watching you — is often a bid for stimulation or attention.

🗣️ Attention-seeking & excessive meowing

Constant vocalising, pawing, following you around, or “naughty” behaviour that reliably gets a reaction can all mean an unmet need for engagement and play.

🌙 Night-time zoomies & restlessness

Energy that isn't spent during the day comes out at night — racing around, yowling, and pouncing on you in bed. It's usually a sign of too little daytime activity.

😾 Anxious or “off” behaviour

Boredom can tip into anxiety, hiding, or even aggression. These can have other causes too, so watch for patterns and get advice if they persist.

Boredom or something medical?

This is the most important step. Many boredom signs overlap with illness, so never assume boredom for a sign that appears suddenly, severely, or with other symptoms. Use this as a rough guide — not a diagnosis.

⚠️ When in doubt, check it out

Overgrooming, appetite changes, lethargy and toileting changes can all be caused by pain or illness. If a behaviour is new, worsening, or paired with other symptoms, see your vet before treating it as “just boredom.”

How to fix boredom

Once your vet has ruled out medical causes, boredom is very fixable — and usually quickly:

  • Daily interactive play. Two or three short wand-toy sessions that let your cat stalk, chase and catch — ending with a treat or meal.
  • Make food a job. Use puzzle feeders or scatter-feed so mealtimes involve hunting.
  • Add vertical space. Cat trees, shelves and window perches expand the world and give “cat TV.”
  • Rotate toys weekly so they feel new again.
  • Provide scratching outlets to burn energy and relieve stress.
  • Keep a routine and consider a second cat only if temperament and slow introductions allow.
  • Ruled out medical causes with a vet
  • Interactive play every day
  • At least one puzzle feeder or scatter-feed
  • Vertical space and a window view
  • A sturdy scratching post
  • Toys rotated to stay novel

✓ The takeaway

A bored cat isn't a lost cause — it's a cat asking for more to do. Rule out illness first, then add daily play, food puzzles and things to climb. Most “bad” behaviours fade once the boredom behind them is met.

Preparing for a Kitten

A little planning before your kitten arrives makes the first days calm instead of chaotic. Here's exactly what to buy, how to set up a safe room, how to kitten-proof your home, and what to sort out with the vet.

⏱️ 8 min read
Updated 7 July 2026
First published 7 July 2026
Reviewed for Cat&Me Kitten Care

Bringing home a kitten is one of the most exciting days there is — and it goes far more smoothly when the essentials are ready and waiting. The goal is simple: have the supplies bought, a quiet safe space set up, hazards removed, and a first vet visit booked before your kitten walks through the door.

💡 The short version

Before pick-up day: buy the core supplies, set up a quiet safe room, kitten-proof every room your kitten will access, and book a first vet check. Bring the same food the kitten is already eating to avoid tummy upsets.

1. The essential supplies checklist

You don't need everything on the pet-shop wall — just the basics done well. Here's what to have ready:

  • Kitten food — a complete food formulated for kittens (buy the same brand/type they're already on).
  • Food & water bowls — shallow ceramic or stainless steel; keep them away from the litter tray.
  • Litter tray + litter — a shallow, low-sided tray and unscented litter to start.
  • A carrier — sturdy and secure for the ride home and future vet trips.
  • Bed & cosy blanket — a warm, washable place to sleep and hide.
  • Scratching post — tall and stable, to protect furniture from day one.
  • Toys — wand toys, small balls and a couple of soft kickers for play.
  • Grooming basics — a soft brush and cat-specific nail clippers.
  • ID — a breakaway collar with a tag, and plan to microchip at the first vet visit.

2. Set up a safe room

A brand-new home is overwhelming for a tiny kitten. Rather than giving them the run of the house, start them in one quiet room — a spare bedroom, office, or even a bathroom works well.

Put everything they need in that room: bed and hiding spots, food and water in one corner, and the litter tray in a separate corner well away from the food. Let your kitten decompress and explore this small space first, then widen their world room by room over the following days.

✓ The 3-3-3 rhythm

Many kittens need about 3 days to feel safe, 3 weeks to settle into a routine, and 3 months to feel fully at home. Don't rush it — patience in week one pays off for years.

3. Kitten-proof your home

Kittens are curious, athletic, and small enough to squeeze almost anywhere. Walk through each room at kitten-eye level and look for trouble:

  • Cords & wires — bundle, tape down or cover cables; tie up blind and curtain cords so nothing dangles.
  • Small objects — pick up hair ties, string, rubber bands and anything swallowable.
  • Gaps & hiding spots — block holes behind appliances and baseboards where a kitten could get stuck.
  • Tall furniture — secure bookshelves and wobbly furniture to the wall; kittens climb everything.
  • Toxic plants — remove lilies (very dangerous to cats), pothos, aloe and similar houseplants.
  • Chemicals & medicines — store cleaning products and pills in closed cupboards.
  • Appliances — keep washing machines, dryers and toilet lids closed.

⚠️ Lilies are an emergency

All parts of true lilies are highly toxic to cats — even a nibble or pollen on the fur can cause kidney failure. Keep them out of the house entirely, and call your vet immediately if your kitten contacts one.

4. Plan the first vet visit

Book a check-up for the first week or two. Your vet will give your kitten a health check and set up the essentials of first-year care:

  • Vaccinations and a schedule for boosters
  • Parasite prevention (worms and fleas)
  • Microchipping for permanent ID
  • A conversation about neutering timing and diet

Bring any paperwork from the breeder or shelter, and use the carrier for the trip so car rides feel normal from the start.

5. The ride home and first day

Keep the car journey calm and quiet, with the carrier secured and lined with familiar-smelling bedding. When you arrive, take your kitten straight to the safe room, open the carrier, and let them come out in their own time. Resist the urge to invite everyone over — quiet, gentle first days build trust fastest.

✓ The takeaway

Supplies bought, safe room ready, home kitten-proofed, vet booked. Nail those four and your kitten's first days will be calm, safe and happy — the perfect start to a long friendship.

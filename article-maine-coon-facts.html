Maine Coon Facts

The Maine Coon is one of the largest domestic cat breeds and one of the most beloved — a shaggy-coated “gentle giant” with a sociable, dog-like personality. Here's what makes this American original so special.

⏱️ 8 min read
Updated 8 July 2026
First published 8 July 2026
Reviewed for Cat&Me

Few cats turn heads like a Maine Coon. With their impressive size, luxurious coat, tufted ears and bushy tail, they look like miniature wild cats — but they're famous for being affectionate, playful and endlessly friendly. Here are the key facts about this classic breed.

💡 Quick summary

Origin: United States (Maine). Size: one of the largest domestic breeds. Coat: long, thick, shaggy. Temperament: gentle, sociable, playful. Lifespan: roughly 9–15 years. Grooming: regular — several times a week.

1. They're one of the largest domestic cats

Maine Coons are famous for their size. Males can weigh anywhere from around 5.5–8 kg (12–18 lb) or more, while females are typically 4–6 kg. They're long, muscular and heavily boned, with some reaching well over a metre from nose to tail tip. Unusually, they're slow growers — many don't reach full size until they're three to five years old.

2. The “gentle giant” personality

Despite their imposing size, Maine Coons are sweet-tempered and gentle. They're highly sociable, bond closely with their families, and are often described as dog-like — many follow their owners from room to room, play fetch, and even enjoy water. They're intelligent and playful throughout life, yet rarely demanding or clingy.

Another endearing quirk: for such a big cat, they have a surprisingly soft, quiet voice, often chirping and trilling rather than meowing loudly.

3. A true American original

The Maine Coon is considered the only longhair breed native to the United States, and is the official state cat of Maine. Its exact origins are the stuff of legend, but the most likely story is that long-haired cats brought over by seafarers bred with local shorthaired cats in New England, producing a hardy, weather-proof working cat that kept barns and ships free of rodents. That rugged history explains the thick, water-resistant coat and sturdy frame.

✓ Fun fact

Some Maine Coons are polydactyl — born with extra toes. It's a harmless inherited trait that was once common in the breed's working ancestors, as big “snowshoe” paws helped them get around.

4. That coat needs regular care

The Maine Coon's shaggy, semi-long coat is beautiful but does need attention. Brushing and combing several times a week — ideally getting them used to it from kittenhood — keeps the fur from tangling and matting, especially on the belly and behind the legs. Regular grooming also cuts down on hairballs and shedding.

5. Health & lifespan

Maine Coons generally live around 9–15 years. Like all pedigree breeds, they're prone to a few inherited conditions, so buying from a responsible breeder who health-tests their cats is important.

⚠️ Health issues to be aware of

Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) is the most significant — a heart-muscle disease the breed is genetically predisposed to. Others include hip dysplasia, spinal muscular atrophy (SMA), and polycystic kidney disease. Reputable breeders screen for these; regular vet check-ups help catch problems early.

Because they're big cats with hearty appetites, Maine Coons can also gain weight easily — measured meals and plenty of play help keep them in shape.

Is a Maine Coon right for you?

  • You have space for a large, active cat
  • You can commit to brushing several times a week
  • You want a sociable, playful, people-loving companion
  • You can budget for a bigger cat's food and vet care
  • You'll choose a health-testing breeder or consider adoption

✓ The takeaway

The Maine Coon combines striking looks with a genuinely gentle, friendly nature, making it a favourite family cat. Be ready for its size, appetite and grooming needs, choose a responsible source, and you'll have a devoted “gentle giant” for years.
